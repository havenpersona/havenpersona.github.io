About

Machine Learning is one of the most exciting things that I have ever learned. Among sub-fields of artificial intelligence, I would summarize my research interest as natural language processing for music. (I am one of the researchers who understand that language is the key that helps connection among music-lovers, and among musicians.) I am very lucky to be advised by Juhan Nam for my research at Music and Audio Computing Lab at KAIST. Before coming here, I gained my bachelor's degree in Interdisciplinary Computing and the Arts from the University of California San Diego where I graduated magna cum laude with ICAM Research Award. For my side-projects (mostly for classes), I have developed some VR applications that leverage spatial audio.

Besides research, I like teaching. I have taught

  • Mathematics for Machine Learning and Natural Language Processing on Youtube.
  • Korean in a language school in Tokyo, Japan.
  • Music Productuon to elementary school students from Dangjin.

Below are the things that I learned so far.

  • Machine Learning - Pytorch, Tensorflow, Weights&Biases (GCT 634, AI605)
  • Web Scraping - Scrapy Splash Selenium (Udemy)
  • Audio Signal Processing - Scipy and Librosa (GCT534, MUS171, MUS176)
  • Virtual Reality Development - Unity(GCT742)
  • Natural Languages - Korean(native), English, Japanese(JLPT N1)
  • Music Production and Composition - Ableton Live mainly (MUS 173, MUS174)
